Pabuhat / Pabigat: These terms may have emerged from Filipino gamers, with "pabuhat" referring to someone who is being carried or carried someone in the game (getting a higher-ranked player to boost their rank) while "pabigat" refer to someone who is a burden or dragging the team down. - Pabuhat: "Nakarating ako sa Diamond rank sa tulong ng kaibigan na pabuhat sa akin."
- Pabigat: "Ayoko maglaro kasama yung player na yun, sobrang pabigat sa team."
Meta: Refers to the most effective tactics available in a game. It could have been adopted from English gaming terminology. Ang kasalukuyang Meta sa laro ay nagbibigay advantage sa laro na binabago o pinapalakas ang hero na gagamitin
Ig/og: Could stand for "in-game/out-game," used to differentiate between actions or events happening within the game and outside of it. og/ig pa ako ngayon pwede mamaya nalang tayo kumain.
Cancer: term has been used in gaming to describe something extremely unpleasant or toxic within the game. ayusin mo naman laro mo lagi kang nag papa feed sa kalaban cancer ka naman masyado sa laro mo.
Noob: Derived from "newbie," refers to someone inexperienced or a beginner. bakit ganyan ka maglaro napaka noob mo maglaro pwede ka naman mag practice mode muna bago sumabak sa rank matches
EZ: Short for "easy," used to taunt opponents after an easy win. ano batong mga kalaban nato ang hihina tapos agad laban Ez Win
Ggwp/g/ncg: "Good game, well played" or variations of it, used as a sportsmanlike gesture after a match. "NCG" might mean "no contest, gg." GGWP guys
Dumbell: Is used when telling someone that they are “dumb” or they made a mistake. Nakalimutan mong bumili ng potions? Wag kang maging dumbell
Arat: Reversed word for “Tara” which means “Lets go!”. Arat compshop
Umay: means sick or getting tired of through something. Umay:*- mamaya na ko maglalaro kayo muna na uumay nako

Cancel: Refers to the act of boycotting or withdrawing support for someone due to their controversial actions or statements. icacancel ko nato sa mga pinapanood ko sa facebook dahil nag propromote ng sugal
Naols: A colloquial way of saying “I hope I experience that”. binigyan ako ng topfan badge ni idol ivana alawi” “naols binigyan ng topfan idol ko din siya eh
Shesh: An exclamation, probably derived from "sheesh," expressing surprise or disbelief. shesh grabi pormahan oh TUPAD CLOTHING.
Ekis: Means when something is not right or disagreeing with something. ekis kana sakin ayoko sa sinungaling na tao
Bonak: Refers to when calling someone is Dumb. Huwag mangtawag ng bonak panatilihin natin ang respeto sa usapan.
Pitik: Refer to clicking or tapping, similar to giving likes or reactions. Pinipitik ko ang like button sa lahat ng larawan mo; ang galing!
Genggeng: A made up word to call someone looking like a “gangster” or arrogant. Huwag husgahan ang tao base sa itsura; ang tawag ng genggeng ay hindi makatarungan

The "Racist" category includes slurs and offensive terms targeting groups based on race, ethnicity, skin color, national origin, etc. This language can be extremely hurtful and promote harmful stereotypes. We urge visitors to refrain from using these racist terms in any context.
